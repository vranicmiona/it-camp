Berry spirulina smoothie

Try this recipe – mix berries, baby spinach, and spirulina to get an anti-inflammatory smoothie, reducing inflammations in the body. Blueberries and blackberries, baby spinach, and spirulina contain antioxidants that prevent oxidative damage. Enjoy! Preparation time: 15 minutes Servings: 2-3 Ingredients: 1 banana ¾ cup blueberries ¾ cup blackberries 1 ½ cup unsweetened almond milk..

Spirulina is a blue-green algae grown in fresh water around the world, usually cultivated under an abundance of sunlight. It is known for its high plant-based protein content, offering ALL of the essential amino acids. 3 tablespoons of spirulina is equivalent to the protein content of 2 eggs! It contains a high amount of vitamins and minerals, as well as essential fatty acids such as gamma linoleic acid. It binds itself to toxins in the body to help with detoxification and some smaller studies have shown that it boosts and supports the immune system. Mind the ocean-like flavour, spirulina is a go-to in our house for that afternoon pick me up, supplying some good energy and optimal nourishment. Pair it with antioxidant-packed blueberries and some healthy fats from nuts and seeds for a smoothie that packs a punc.
